Following the opening prayers, we recognized a minute of silence in honor of the late Consul General Colin Mayers.
The meeting highlights for this month are as follows:

  • The funeral service of the late Consul General of Barbados, the Honorable Colin Mayers, will take place at 11:00 a.m. on Saturday, April 21, 2018 at the Holy Sacrament Episcopal Church, 2801 North University Drive, Pembroke Pines, FL 33024. The CFL Bajans will be represented by our president Dr. Dale Husbands, and member Mr. Ryvan Campbell. Other members are invited to attend.
    Please keep the family of the Consul General in your prayers.

  • We welcomed one guest who is a relative of two of our members from Carrington’s Village.

  • The president thanked all the members who volunteered at the Second Harvest event earlier that morning.

  • The monetary donation to the Red Foundation in Barbados was made on behalf of the organization by the President. The Red Foundation as a charitable organization in Barbados is dedicated to:
    • The relief of poverty
    • The advancement in education
    • The provision of social welfare services to those in need
    • The promotion and encouragement of projects for community development
    It was formed by Mr. Stedson Wiltshire a.k.a. Red Plastic Bag (Bag) and some of his associates from relevant professions.
    Their current projects include refurbishing St. Mark’s Primary School in St. Philip, Barbados, and plans for this school year (2018-2019) to select two (2) students from each primary school in Barbados to receive a backpack filled with school supplies and a computer. This initiative is to be completed annually.

    Some Bajan trivia - 'How well do you remember'

    By: Marjorie McCauley, a CFL Bajan

    Can you remember these aspects of old Barbados?

    The former name for a district hospital?
    Answer: almshouse

    What we used to call a toilet?
    Answer: chamber

    What was the previous name of the Sir Grantley Adams International Airport?
    Answer: Seawell

    The name for a straw hat worn by school children years ago.
    Answer: Panama hat

    Today we say chickens, in the olden days they said ____.
    Answer: yard fowl

    A stiff undergarment worn by woment in the olden days.
    Answer: a can-can

    A moveable cupboard used for storing food.
    Answer: a larder

    The hat once worn by the plantation owners.
    Answer: cork hat

    'Yuh know, Patsy did always a fair weather friend'. What does that mean?
    Answer: She's unreliable person

    'Yuh tell me, why would anybody let a 14-year old back back a car?'. What is the 14-year old doing to the car?
    Answer: reversing
