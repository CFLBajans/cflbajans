The meeting highlights for this month are as follows:

  • We welcomed several visitors:
    • two visitors from Apopka, FL by way of St. George & St. Michael Barbados.
    • one visitor from New York by way of Trinidad
    • one visitor who is a representative of Bajan Diaspora Online.

  • The president thanked all the members who participated in the Taste of Barbados event in April. He reported it as being a successful event.
    Next year (2019) the Board hopes to have a bigger event conducted in a different format. Members' and friends of members' assistance will be solicited to assist with this.

  • We also discussed the upcoming gala November 10th, 2018 at The Rosen Center. The venue and entertainment have been secured. Sponsorship forms are now available for Journal ads. Persons are reminded that ads are available for companies in Florida as well as Barbados, and personal ads e.g. celebrating marriage, are also available. Please encourage your friends and interested persons to take out an ad in our journal.

  • The corresponding secretary indicated the following upcoming events from our friends.
    • An invitation from Russel Home for their open house to view their new facilities.
    • An invitation for Mother’s Day Dinner on May 13th, 2018 @ Allure House at International Drive from 5-12pm

  • On behalf of the Board Members, President Dale Husbands wished all mothers A Happy Mother’s Day!

  • Instead of the Barbados' Trivia session, two members presented on Hurricane Preparedness. Topics covered included the definitions of the hurricanes, tropical storms, and tropical cyclones as well as the categories, the impacts of tropical storms and hurricanes, updates to the National Hurricane Center's products for this year, the various products and their intended purpose, preparing and executing family plans and general behaviors before, during and after hurricanes.

REMINDERS:

  • The CFL Bajans' bus ride to Fort Desoto Park is slated for May 19th, 2018. The buses will leave from the Caribbean Super Center, West Colonial Drive, Orlando, FL. Tickets for the event are $25. Please contact the president at cflbajans dot com or the corresponding secretary at cflbajans dot com to secure your spot on the bus!

  • CFL Bajans upcoming volunteer/community activities:
      July 28th 2018 8:15am – 11:30am, Second Harvest Food Bank of Central Florida, 411 Mercy Dr, Orlando, FL 32805. You must wear closed in shoes for this activity. A sign-up sheet will be circulating at the next meeting. Also, please feel free to contact the secretary at cflbajans dot com or the corresponding secretary at cflbajans dot com to sign up for the volunteer activity if interested.

  • Please keep our group members in your prayers especially those who are ill and/or mourning the loss of love ones.

    Some Bajan trivia - 'How well do you remember'

    By: Marjorie McCauley, a CFL Bajan

    Can you remember these aspects of old Barbados?

    The former name for a district hospital?
    Answer: almshouse

    What we used to call a toilet?
    Answer: chamber

    What was the previous name of the Sir Grantley Adams International Airport?
    Answer: Seawell

    The name for a straw hat worn by school children years ago.
    Answer: Panama hat

    Today we say chickens, in the olden days they said ____.
    Answer: yard fowl

    A stiff undergarment worn by women in the olden days.
    Answer: a can-can

    A moveable cupboard used for storing food.
    Answer: a larder

    The hat once worn by the plantation owners.
    Answer: cork hat

    'Yuh know, Patsy did always a fair weather friend'. What does that mean?
    Answer: She's unreliable person

    'Yuh tell me, why would anybody let a 14-year old back back a car?'. What is the 14-year old doing to the car?
    Answer: reversing
