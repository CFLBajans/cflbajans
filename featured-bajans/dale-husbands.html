Dale Husbands, PhD.

Dr. Dale Husbands who was born in Barbados, West Indies, migrated to the United States of America in 1975 and settled in Brooklyn, New York. In 1980, Mr. Husbands joined the United States Air Force and worked as a Maintenance Management Information Systems Computer Specialist until his separation from the military in 1989. He subsequently worked for Lucerne Valley Unified School District as a Computer Coordinator, and taught computer science courses as an adjunct professor at Victor Valley Community College in Victorville, California.
Dr. Husbands received his doctorate in Computer Information Technology from Nova Southeastern University and also holds a Masters Degree in Information Systems and a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science.
Dr. Husbands is currently serving as Professor of Information Technology at Valencia College. In July 2014 he resigned his position as the Dean of Business and Technical Programs at the Osceola Campus of Valencia College. He served in this position from 2001 to 2014. In this role, he was responsible for the supervision of 23 full-time faculty and staff, over 75 adjunct faculty, two National Science Foundation grant workers, and seven computer labs. The department serves over 15,000 students annually. Degree programs under his supervision include: Computer Programming and Analysis, Office Systems Technology, Database Management, Computer Information Technology, Accounting Technology, Business Administration, Culinary Management, Hospitality and Tourism, Restaurant Management, Real Estate, and Criminal Justice.
Dr. Husbands started his career at Valencia Community College in September 1990, as a computer lab manager and adjunct computer science instructor. He was also responsible for the daily operations of the finance office at the Osceola Campus.Dr. Husbands held this position until 1993, when he was hired as a tenure-track faculty member in the Computer Programming and Analysis and became tenured 1996. He has also served as the Director of the Computer of the Computer Programming and Analysis program at Valencia Community College.
During his tenure at Valencia Community College, Dr. Husbands has been actively involved in many college-wide committees including Curriculum, Educational Technology, Equal Access Equal Opportunity, and Economic Development. He served as the Osceola Campus Faculty Senate president from 1997 to 1999, and as a team leader of the Title III Development team. Dr. Husbands has also served on several selection and interview committees for various new position openings and participated in various state and federal grants at Valencia Community College. Dr. Husbands has received numerous awards during his educational and professional careers including the National Chair for Excellence award in Education.
He is married to the Angela Husbands who works as a Physical Therapist Assistant at Florida Hospital, and has a son Shane.
